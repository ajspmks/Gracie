Gracie Abrams

Born and raised in Los Angeles County, California, Abrams is the daughter of J. J. Abrams, a film director, and Katie McGrath, a film and television producer. In October 2019, Abrams made her debut in the music industry with the release of her first single, "Mean It", under Interscope Records. The following year, on July 14, 2020, she dropped her debut EP titled "Minor," which featured several hit singles like "I miss you, I'm sorry" and "21." Abrams collaborated with acclaimed producers such as Joel Little and Blake Slatkin on this project.

Continuing her successful journey, on March 24, 2021, Abrams teamed up with Benny Blanco for a new single called "Unlearn," which became part of Blanco's album "Friends Keep Secrets 2." On May 7, 2021, Abrams released another single, "Mess It Up," accompanied by a music video. In October 2021, Abrams treated her fans to the single "Feels Like" along with its music video. Shortly after, she released "Rockland," a collaborative effort with Aaron Dessner. The announcement of her second commercial project, titled "This Is What It Feels Like," followed in the subsequent month. The EP, released on November 12, 2021, included the previously released singles "Feels Like" and "Rockland." To promote the EP, Abrams embarked on the "This Is What It Feels Like Tour," which started on February 2, 2022, in Salt Lake City and concluded on May 31, 2022, in Stockholm. In addition to her headlining tour, she also joined Olivia Rodrigo as the supporting act for her "Sour Tour," where she performed songs from her latest project. Abrams continued to captivate her audience with new music releases, including "Block Me Out" in April 2022 and "Difficult" in October 2022. The latter served as the lead single for her debut album. On January 9, 2023, she announced her first full-length album, "Good Riddance," which hit the shelves on February 24, 2023. Preceding the album release, she dropped the second single, "Where Do We Go Now?", on January 13, followed by the third single, "Amelie," on February 10. Abrams also delighted her fans with a deluxe edition of the album on April 24, 2023, featuring the single "Block Me Out" along with three new tracks: "Unsteady," "405," and "Two People." In 2023, Abrams graced the stage as an opening act for selected shows on Taylor Swift's Eras Tour in the United States. Additionally, she embarked on her third headlining concert tour, the "Good Riddance Tour," further solidifying her status as a rising star in the music industry.
